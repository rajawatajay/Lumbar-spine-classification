About Lumbar Spine Degeneration Detection

Using Deep Learning to provide accurate and efficient diagnosis of lumbar spine degeneration.

Project Overview

This project leverages Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to detect and classify lumbar spine degeneration using advanced medical imaging datasets. With thousands of annotated spinal MRI and X-ray images, the system is designed to identify degenerative conditions such as disc herniation, spinal stenosis, and osteoarthritis.

Key Features

  • Integration of spine MRI and X-ray datasets for comprehensive analysis.
  • Automatic detection and classification of degenerative spine conditions.
  • Advanced CNN architectures to enhance diagnostic precision.
  • Robust preprocessing techniques to manage noise, class imbalance, and large image resolutions.

Objective

The primary aim is to assist radiologists and orthopedic specialists by providing a reliable tool for early detection and assessment of lumbar spine degeneration. This project aims to reduce diagnostic delays and improve patient outcomes.

Impact

The Lumbar Spine Degeneration project is a step toward enhancing diagnostic accuracy in spinal health. With deep learning-driven insights, the system has the potential to support faster, more consistent clinical decisions and contribute to better spine care worldwide.
